BMI Body Mass Index: a ratio of your weight to height. It gives a rough indicator of whether you're in a healthy weight range, though it doesn't account for muscle mass.
Body Fat The percentage of your body that is fat. Estimated here using your BMI and age. Lower is generally healthier, but some fat is essential for normal body function.
BMR Basal Metabolic Rate: the number of calories your body burns at complete rest just to keep you alive (breathing, circulation, etc.).
TDEE Total Daily Energy Expenditure: your BMR multiplied by your activity level. This is roughly how many calories you burn in a day. Eat below this to lose weight, above it to gain.
Ideal Weight A healthy weight range for your height based on the Devine formula. Use it as a general target, not a strict rule — body composition matters more than the number on the scale.
